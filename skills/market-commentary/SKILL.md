# market-commentary

Generates a professional weekly or monthly market commentary for client communications. Provides a narrative framework the advisor fills with current data — saves 2–3 hours of writing per week.

## How to Invoke

Say: *"Write a market commentary for [week/month]"* — then provide the key events to cover (Fed decisions, earnings, economic data, geopolitical events). The more context you provide, the less placeholder completion is required.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Period & Events

Confirm:

- **Period**: weekly, monthly, or quarterly
- **Key events** (advisor provides): Fed decisions, CPI/PCE data, jobs report, earnings season themes, geopolitical events, market moves worth noting
- **Tone calibration**: measured, calm, authoritative — never alarmist; always anchored to long-term strategy

### Step 2 — Commentary Structure

Produce a full commentary with four named sections:

**The Macro View**
Economic backdrop narrative. Advisor provides the numbers (GDP growth, CPI, unemployment rate, Fed funds rate); the skill frames the narrative interpretation. Example structure:
> "The U.S. economy [continued to show resilience / showed signs of softening] in [period], with [DATA POINT] and [DATA POINT]. The Federal Reserve [held rates steady / raised / cut] at its [month] meeting, citing [ADVISOR TO COMPLETE]."

**What Markets Did**
Structured narrative covering:
- U.S. equities (S&P 500, Nasdaq, small-cap — advisor fills returns)
- Fixed income (10-year Treasury yield, investment-grade spreads)
- International markets (developed / emerging — advisor fills)
- Alternatives / commodities if relevant

**What It Means**
Interpretation for long-term investors. Key message: short-term noise vs. long-term signal. Reframe volatility, reinforce discipline.

**What We're Watching**
2–3 forward-looking themes with brief explanation of why they matter. Mark as `[ADVISOR TO CONFIRM/ADJUST]`.

### Step 3 — Tone Calibration

All versions: measured, calm, authoritative. Never say "the market crashed" — say "markets experienced elevated volatility." Never forecast with certainty. Never alarm. Always return to long-term strategy.

### Step 4 — Client Segment Versions

Produce two versions of the full commentary:

- **Conservative Client Version**: emphasize capital preservation, income stability, diversification benefits, portfolio resilience. Minimize discussion of growth/upside. Frame volatility as managed.
- **Growth Client Version**: emphasize long-term opportunity, positioning for recovery, the case for staying invested, compounding over time. More forward-looking tone.

### Step 5 — Social & Email

- **LinkedIn Post**: 250 words max. Professional, thought-leadership tone. Hook in first line. No jargon. End with a call to reach out or a reflective question.
- **Email Subject Lines**: 3 options ranging from informational to curiosity-driven.
